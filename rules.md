# Matcher Platform Rules & Guidelines

**Last Updated:** July 22, 2026

Matcher exists to make brand-influencer partnerships easier, fairer, and more transparent. These rules apply to everyone on the platform—brands, agencies, and individual creators—and exist to protect the trust that makes Matcher work. By creating an account, you agree to follow them.

If anything here is unclear, reach out to our support team before you run into an issue, not after.

---

## 1. Account Creation & Verification

### Who Can Join
- Users must be **18 years or older** to create a Matcher account. Influencers under 18 may only participate through a verified parent or legal guardian account.
- Brands must represent a legitimate business, agency, or organization with a valid business email or company website.
- Influencers must represent an active, genuine social media presence they own and operate.

### Verification Requirements
- **Influencers** must connect at least one social platform account (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or equivalent) through Matcher's official OAuth integration—not manual entry—so audience data can be verified directly from the source.
- **Brands** must verify a company domain email address or provide business registration details for accounts requesting access to advanced discovery and payment features.
- Profile information (name, handles, follower counts, contact details) must be accurate and kept up to date. Significant changes—such as switching primary platforms or company ownership—should be reflected within 14 days.
- Matcher reserves the right to request additional verification at any time, including for accounts flagged by our fraud detection systems.

### One Account Per Person or Entity
- Each individual or brand entity may hold **one active Matcher account** per role (brand or influencer). Duplicate accounts created to evade suspension, manipulate matching, or misrepresent audience size are not permitted.

## 2. Prohibited Behavior

The following are not allowed anywhere on Matcher, including profiles, messages, campaign briefs, and content submissions:

- **Fake or inflated profiles** — Using purchased followers, bots, engagement pods, or other artificial means to misrepresent audience size or engagement.
- **Impersonation** — Creating a profile as, or pretending to be, another person, brand, or organization.
- **Spam and unsolicited outreach** — Mass-messaging users outside the intent of a genuine partnership inquiry, or using Matcher to promote unrelated services.
- **Circumventing the platform** — Using Matcher solely to identify a contact and then redirecting the entire relationship, negotiation, and payment off-platform to avoid fees or accountability. Initial discovery and connection should occur through Matcher.
- **Scams and fraud** — Requesting payment for services not rendered, offering counterfeit or misrepresented products, phishing for personal or financial information, or any other deceptive financial conduct.
- **Misleading claims** — Misrepresenting campaign results, audience demographics, follower authenticity, or brand affiliation.
- **Undisclosed paid partnerships** — Influencers must disclose sponsored content in accordance with applicable advertising regulations (e.g., FTC guidelines in the U.S., ASA in the UK, or equivalent local law) using appropriate tags or disclosures.
- **Harassment or abuse** — Threatening, discriminatory, or abusive behavior directed at other users or Matcher staff.

Accounts found engaging in any of the above are subject to warnings, restriction, or permanent removal as outlined in Section 5.

## 3. Collaboration Expectations

Matcher facilitates the relationship, but the quality of a partnership depends on both sides holding up their end. The following are baseline expectations, not guarantees—specific terms should always be confirmed in each campaign agreement.

### Communication & Response Times
- Both parties are expected to respond to active campaign inquiries and messages within **3 business days**. Accounts that are consistently unresponsive may see reduced visibility in matching results.
- If a party is no longer interested in a conversation or opportunity, a brief decline is preferred over silence—it helps keep the marketplace efficient for everyone.

### Deliverables
- Campaign briefs (from brands) should clearly state deliverables, timelines, usage rights, and compensation before an influencer is expected to accept or begin work.
- Influencers are expected to deliver agreed-upon content by the agreed deadline, or communicate delays as early as possible.
- Any changes to scope, timeline, or deliverables after initial agreement should be documented in writing within the Matcher platform (campaign messages or contract tool) to maintain a clear record for both parties.

### Payment Terms
- Payment terms (amount, schedule, and method) must be agreed upon **before** content is created or published.
- Brands are expected to pay influencers within the timeframe specified in the campaign agreement, and no later than **30 days** from deliverable approval unless otherwise agreed in writing.
- Influencers are expected to deliver content that reasonably matches what was agreed upon in the brief before requesting payment.
- Matcher's optional integrated payment and escrow tools are the recommended way to handle transactions, as they provide a documented record in case of a dispute.

## 4. Content Standards

All content shared on Matcher—profiles, campaign briefs, portfolio samples, messages, and any media uploaded to the platform—must comply with the following:

- **No illegal content** — Content promoting or depicting illegal activity, counterfeit goods, or unlicensed regulated products (e.g., certain pharmaceuticals, weapons, or controlled substances) is prohibited.
- **No hate speech or discrimination** — Content that attacks or demeans individuals or groups based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics is not permitted.
- **No sexually explicit content** — Matcher is a professional collaboration platform and is not intended for adult content campaigns.
- **No graphic violence or harmful content** — Including content that promotes self-harm, violence, or dangerous activities.
- **No misinformation** — Campaigns and content should not knowingly spread false or misleading claims, particularly in sensitive categories such as health, finance, or safety.
- **Respect intellectual property** — Only upload or share content you own or have explicit rights to use. Do not misuse brand assets, logos, or trademarks outside the terms of an agreed campaign.

Matcher may remove content that violates these standards and, depending on severity, take action on the associated account.

## 5. Dispute Resolution & Account Suspension

### Disputes Between Users
- If a disagreement arises over deliverables, payment, or campaign terms, both parties should first attempt to resolve it directly, using the documented campaign agreement as the reference point.
- If a resolution can't be reached, either party may open a dispute through Matcher Support. Disputes are reviewed using platform records (messages, briefs, delivery timestamps, and payment records where applicable).
- Matcher acts as a neutral facilitator in disputes and will make a determination based on available evidence; this may include withholding or releasing escrowed payments, where our payment tools were used.

### Enforcement Approach
Matcher generally takes a graduated approach to enforcement, except in cases of severe or repeated violations (such as fraud, harassment, or illegal content), which may result in immediate suspension:

1. **Warning** — A notice explaining the violation and requested corrective action.
2. **Restriction** — Temporary limits on messaging, visibility in matching, or campaign participation.
3. **Suspension** — Temporary loss of account access pending review.
4. **Termination** — Permanent removal from the platform for serious or repeated violations.

### Appeals
- Users who believe an enforcement action was made in error may submit an appeal through Matcher Support within 30 days of the action. Appeals are reviewed by a person, not solely automated systems.

## 6. Data Privacy & Security

- Matcher collects the information necessary to operate the platform: profile details, verified social account data, campaign communications, and payment information (processed through our PCI-compliant payment partner—Matcher does not store raw payment card data).
- Audience and engagement data pulled from connected social accounts is used only for matching, verification, and analytics purposes, and is not sold to third parties.
- Users can request a copy of their data or request account deletion at any time through account settings or by contacting Support, in accordance with applicable data protection law (e.g., GDPR, CCPA).
- Direct messages and campaign details shared within Matcher are visible only to the participating parties and, where relevant, Matcher's support/dispute team.
- All data in transit is encrypted (HTTPS/TLS); see Matcher's full Privacy Policy for details on data retention, storage, and third-party processors.

## 7. Eligibility, Geography & Audience Requirements

- Matcher is currently available to brands and influencers located in supported regions, listed at signup; availability may expand over time.
- There is **no strict minimum follower count** required for influencers to join, but campaign matching for brand-funded partnerships generally favors accounts with:
  - A minimum of **1,000 followers** on at least one connected platform, and
  - Verifiable, authentic engagement (as assessed by Matcher's audience verification tools).
- Nano and emerging creators below this threshold may still create a profile and participate in unpaid or product-based collaborations, and can become eligible for paid campaign matching as their verified audience grows.
- Brands of any size are welcome, from individual founders to enterprise marketing teams; certain advanced features (bulk campaign tools, dedicated support) may be limited to specific pricing tiers.
- Matcher does not restrict participation by industry, provided the brand's products, services, and content comply with Section 4 (Content Standards) and applicable law.

## 8. Platform Fees & Commission

- **For Influencers:** Matcher does not charge influencers a fee to create a profile or apply to open campaigns. A **service fee (currently 10%)** is deducted from payments processed through Matcher's integrated payment tool, covering payment processing, escrow protection, and dispute support.
- **For Brands:** Access to core discovery and matching tools is available through Matcher's subscription plans (see Pricing). Payments made to influencers through Matcher's platform may include a **transaction processing fee**, disclosed at checkout before a payment is confirmed.
- Fees are always shown transparently before a transaction or subscription is confirmed—there are no hidden deductions.
- Fee structures may be updated from time to time; active users will be notified in advance of any changes that affect existing agreements.
