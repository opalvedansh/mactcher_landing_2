# Product Requirements Document: Matcher Landing Page

**Document Owner:** Product Management
**Status:** Draft for Design & Engineering Review
**Last Updated:** July 22, 2026

---

## 1. Product Overview

**Matcher** is a two-sided SaaS platform that connects brands with influencers through intelligent, data-driven matching. Matcher removes the guesswork, spreadsheets, and cold outreach that dominate influencer marketing today, replacing them with a streamlined discovery and partnership engine.

### Core Value Proposition

| Segment | Value Proposition |
|---|---|
| **Brands** | Discover and vet the right influencers for any campaign in minutes, not weeks—backed by audience data, engagement analytics, and brand-fit scoring. |
| **Influencers** | Get matched with brand partnerships that actually fit your audience, niche, and values—so you can grow income without compromising authenticity. |

The landing page is the primary conversion vehicle for both segments and must clearly communicate value to each without diluting the message for either.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

### Pain Points for Brands
- **Discovery is manual and slow.** Marketing teams rely on manual social media searches, spreadsheets, and word-of-mouth to find influencers.
- **Lack of trustworthy data.** Follower counts are vanity metrics; brands struggle to assess real engagement, audience authenticity, and brand alignment.
- **Fragmented workflows.** Outreach, negotiation, contracts, and performance tracking happen across disconnected tools (email, DMs, spreadsheets).
- **Wasted budget on poor fits.** Mismatched partnerships lead to low ROI, inauthentic content, and reputational risk.
- **No standardized ROI measurement.** Hard to prove campaign performance to leadership.

### Pain Points for Influencers
- **Inconsistent income.** Reliance on inbound DMs and inconsistent brand outreach makes income unpredictable.
- **Misaligned partnerships.** Influencers are pitched products/brands that don't match their values or audience, risking follower trust.
- **Opaque negotiation.** Lack of visibility into fair market rates for their niche and audience size.
- **Time-consuming admin.** Manually pitching brands, negotiating terms, and chasing payments takes time away from content creation.
- **Limited discoverability.** Especially for micro/niche creators, it's hard to be found by relevant brands outside their existing network.

## 3. Goals & Success Metrics

### Landing Page Goals
1. Clearly communicate Matcher's value to two distinct audiences within the first 5 seconds of landing.
2. Drive qualified sign-ups (brand demo requests + influencer applications).
3. Build credibility and trust through social proof, data, and transparent product previews.
4. Segment visitors early so each sees a tailored path (brand vs. influencer).
5. Minimize friction from landing to conversion (sign-up, demo booking, or waitlist join).

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target / Purpose |
|---|---|
| Visitor → Sign-up conversion rate | Primary KPI; benchmark against SaaS landing page industry avg (~3–5%), target 6%+ |
| Segment selection rate (Brand vs. Influencer) | % of visitors who self-identify within first interaction |
| CTA click-through rate (hero + sticky nav) | Measures message resonance |
| Scroll depth / section engagement | Identify which sections drive engagement vs. drop-off |
| Demo request completion rate (brands) | Funnel health for higher-intent B2B path |
| Influencer application start/completion rate | Funnel health for creator-side onboarding |
| Bounce rate | Below 45% target |
| Page load time (LCP) | Under 2.5s on mobile and desktop |
| A/B test lift on hero messaging/CTA | Iterative optimization metric |

## 4. User Personas

### Persona 1: "Brand Brooke" — Brand Marketing Manager
- **Role:** Mid-level marketing manager or influencer marketing lead at a DTC or mid-market brand.
- **Goals:** Find vetted influencers quickly, prove campaign ROI, streamline outreach-to-payment workflow.
- **Concerns:** Wasting budget, brand safety, time investment, integration with existing martech stack.
- **Landing behavior:** Scans for proof points (case studies, ROI stats), looks for a demo CTA or free trial.

### Persona 2: "Creator Chris" — Micro-Influencer / Content Creator
- **Role:** Independent creator with 10K–250K followers in a specific niche (fitness, beauty, tech, parenting, etc.).
- **Goals:** Land brand deals aligned with their content, earn predictable income, avoid spammy/irrelevant pitches.
- **Concerns:** Losing audience trust, fair pay, ease of getting started, legitimacy of the platform.
- **Landing behavior:** Looks for "how it works," earning potential, and testimonials from creators like them.

### Persona 3: "Agency Alex" — Influencer Marketing Agency Lead
- **Role:** Manages influencer campaigns on behalf of multiple brand clients.
- **Goals:** Needs scalable tooling to manage multiple campaigns/clients simultaneously.
- **Concerns:** Multi-client management, reporting/export capabilities, pricing at scale.
- **Landing behavior:** Looks for enterprise/agency-specific features or pricing tier; may need a separate nav path or FAQ mention.

### Persona 4: "Enterprise Emma" — Head of Marketing / CMO (Secondary)
- **Role:** Senior decision-maker evaluating Matcher for org-wide adoption.
- **Goals:** Scalability, security/compliance, measurable ROI, integration with existing tools (Shopify, HubSpot, etc.).
- **Landing behavior:** Seeks enterprise trust signals—security badges, case studies with recognizable brand logos, "Book a demo" over self-serve sign-up.

## 5. Landing Page Sections

### 5.1 Navigation Bar
- Logo, anchor links (How it Works, Features, Pricing, Testimonials), and a dual CTA: **"For Brands"** / **"For Influencers"** toggle or dropdown.
- Sticky on scroll with condensed CTA button.

### 5.2 Hero Section
- **Purpose:** Immediately communicate value prop and segment the visitor.
- **Content:** Headline + subheadline addressing both audiences, or a toggle/tab that switches hero copy and imagery between "Brands" and "Influencers" views.
- **CTA:** Primary CTA changes based on selected segment ("Find Influencers" vs. "Find Brand Deals").
- **Visual:** Product screenshot/UI preview or short looping video showing the matching interface.

### 5.3 Trust Bar / Social Proof (Logos)
- **Purpose:** Immediate credibility.
- **Content:** Logos of brands using Matcher, or aggregate stats ("10,000+ influencers," "$5M+ paid to creators," "500+ brand campaigns").

### 5.4 Problem/Solution Section
- **Purpose:** Validate visitor pain points before presenting the product.
- **Content:** Split into two columns or toggled views—"The old way" vs. "The Matcher way"—for brands and influencers respectively.

### 5.5 How It Works
- **Purpose:** Reduce perceived complexity; show the product is easy to adopt.
- **Content:** 3–4 step visual process, tailored per segment:
  - **Brands:** Define campaign → Get AI-matched influencer shortlist → Review data & vet → Launch & track.
  - **Influencers:** Build profile → Get matched to relevant brands → Negotiate & accept → Get paid.

### 5.6 Key Features / Product Deep Dive
- **Purpose:** Showcase differentiated capabilities (see Section 6).
- **Content:** Feature cards or alternating image/text blocks with UI screenshots for each core feature.

### 5.7 Social Proof — Testimonials & Case Studies
- **Purpose:** Build trust through relatable success stories.
- **Content:** Quote cards or video testimonials from both brand marketers and influencers; include quantifiable results (e.g., "3x engagement," "$12K earned in first quarter").

### 5.8 Metrics/Impact Band
- **Purpose:** Reinforce credibility with data.
- **Content:** Animated counters (e.g., total influencers, campaigns launched, average ROI increase).

### 5.9 Pricing Overview (or Pricing Teaser)
- **Purpose:** Set expectations and filter unqualified leads.
- **Content:** Simple tiered pricing summary (Starter/Growth/Enterprise for brands; Free for influencers) with a link to a full pricing page.

### 5.10 FAQ Section
- **Purpose:** Address objections and reduce support burden.
- **Content:** Segmented FAQs (toggle for brand vs. influencer questions)—covers vetting process, fees, data privacy, contract terms.

### 5.11 Final CTA / Conversion Section
- **Purpose:** Last-chance conversion push.
- **Content:** Bold restated value prop with dual CTA buttons ("Book a Demo" / "Apply as a Creator").

### 5.12 Footer
- **Purpose:** Navigation, trust, and compliance.
- **Content:** Sitemap links, social media icons, legal (privacy policy, terms), contact info, newsletter signup.

## 6. Key Features to Highlight

1. **AI-Powered Matching Engine** — Matches brands and influencers based on audience demographics, engagement quality, niche relevance, and brand values—not just follower count.
2. **Verified Audience Analytics** — Fraud/bot detection and authentic engagement scoring to build brand trust.
3. **Brand-Fit & Values Alignment Score** — Helps influencers filter for partnerships that match their personal brand and audience expectations.
4. **Streamlined Campaign Workflow** — End-to-end tooling: discovery → outreach → negotiation → contract → content approval → payment, all in one platform.
5. **Transparent Rate Benchmarking** — Gives influencers visibility into fair market rates by niche, audience size, and platform.
6. **Integrated Payments** — Secure, built-in payment processing and escrow to eliminate chasing invoices.
7. **Performance Analytics & Reporting** — Real-time campaign ROI dashboards for brands (impressions, engagement, conversions, sales attribution where applicable).
8. **Multi-Platform Support** — Works across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and emerging platforms.

These features should be prioritized in the landing page hierarchy based on what drives the most conversion friction reduction—matching engine and verified analytics are likely the strongest hooks for brands; brand-fit scoring and transparent rates are the strongest hooks for influencers.

## 7. Calls-to-Action (CTAs)

### Primary CTAs
| CTA | Audience | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| **"Book a Demo"** | Brands | Hero, nav bar, final CTA section |
| **"Apply as a Creator"** | Influencers | Hero, nav bar, final CTA section |

### Secondary CTAs
| CTA | Audience | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| **"See How It Works"** | Both | Hero (scroll anchor), How It Works section |
| **"View Pricing"** | Brands (primarily) | Pricing teaser section |
| **"Read Success Stories"** | Both | Testimonials section |
| **"Join the Waitlist"** (if pre-launch/limited capacity) | Both | Fallback CTA if primary sign-up is gated |

### CTA Design Notes
- Use distinct button styling/colors for brand vs. influencer CTAs to reinforce segmentation without confusion.
- All primary CTAs should be A/B testable (copy variants: "Book a Demo" vs. "Get Matched," "Apply as a Creator" vs. "Start Earning").
- Sticky mobile CTA bar recommended for long-scroll mobile experience.

## 8. Messaging & Tone

- **Overall tone:** Confident, modern, and approachable—professional enough for brand decision-makers, relatable enough for individual creators.
- **Voice attributes:** Clear, data-informed, empowering, not overly corporate or jargon-heavy.
- **Brand-facing copy:** Slightly more formal, ROI- and efficiency-focused ("Cut influencer discovery time by 70%").
- **Influencer-facing copy:** Warmer, more conversational, growth- and empowerment-focused ("Get discovered by brands that actually get you").
- **Avoid:** Overpromising guaranteed results, generic stock marketing phrases ("synergy," "leverage," "game-changing"), and jargon that alienates non-technical creators.
- **Data-driven claims** (e.g., "$5M+ paid to creators") should always be sourceable/verifiable and updated regularly—avoid stale or invented statistics.

## 9. Technical & Design Considerations

### Responsiveness
- Fully responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile (mobile-first approach recommended given influencer segment likely browsing via mobile).
- Sticky CTA bar for mobile to maintain conversion visibility during scroll.

### Performance
- Target Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) under 2.5s; optimize hero video/images (lazy loading, compressed assets, WebP/AVIF formats).
- Use a CDN for static assets.

### Integrations
- **Analytics:** Google Analytics 4 or equivalent + a product analytics tool (e.g., Amplitude/Mixpanel) for funnel and segment tracking.
- **A/B Testing:** Tool such as Optimizely or a homegrown feature-flag system for hero and CTA experimentation.
- **CRM/Marketing Automation:** Sync demo requests and sign-ups to CRM (e.g., HubSpot/Salesforce) for brand-side lead nurturing.
- **Payments preview (if applicable):** No live payment processing on landing page, but consider linking to trust/security certifications (SOC 2, PCI compliance messaging) if referenced.

### Analytics & Tracking Requirements
- Track segment selection (Brand vs. Influencer) as a custom event on first interaction.
- Event tracking on all CTA clicks, scroll depth per section, video plays (if hero video used), and form starts/completions.
- UTM parameter support for campaign attribution (paid social, influencer referral links, etc.—fitting, given the product).
- Session recording/heatmap tool (e.g., Hotjar) recommended for qualitative UX insight during initial launch phase.

### SEO Considerations
- Semantic HTML structure with proper heading hierarchy (H1 for hero headline, H2 for section titles).
- Meta title/description optimized for both "influencer marketing platform" and "brand partnership platform for creators" search intents.
- Structured data (Organization, FAQ schema) to support rich search snippets.

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance: sufficient color contrast, alt text for all images, keyboard navigability, ARIA labels for interactive toggles (Brand/Influencer switch).
